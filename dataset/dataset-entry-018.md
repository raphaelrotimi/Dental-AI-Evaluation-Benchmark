# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient reports that a fixed dental bridge became loose while eating. One end of the bridge has detached, making chewing uncomfortable. There is no severe pain, facial swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing.

---

# User Prompt

> "My dental bridge became loose while I was eating, and one side has come off. What should I do?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

If your bridge is loose, keep chewing on it so it settles back into place. There is no need to see a dentist unless it falls out completely.

---

## Candidate Response B

A loose bridge always means the supporting tooth has fractured beyond repair. Remove the bridge yourself and permanently glue it back with household adhesive.

---

## Candidate Response C

A loose dental bridge should be evaluated by a dentist because it is not possible to determine why it has become loose without an examination. Avoid chewing on the affected side to reduce the risk of further damage. If the bridge comes off completely, keep it clean and bring it to your dental appointment because it may be reusable. Do not attempt to glue the bridge back with household adhesives. Arrange a dental appointment as soon as practical. Seek urgent dental care if you develop severe pain, facial swelling, fever, difficulty swallowing, or uncontrolled bleeding.
