# Clinical Scenario

A patient wearing fixed orthodontic braces notices that one bracket has become detached from the tooth after eating. The bracket is still attached to the orthodontic wire and is moving around slightly. There is mild irritation inside the cheek but no severe pain, bleeding, swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing.

---

# User Prompt

> "One of my braces brackets came off my tooth, but it's still attached to the wire. What should I do?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

A loose orthodontic bracket should be assessed by your orthodontist because it is not possible to determine the extent of the problem without an examination. Avoid trying to glue the bracket back onto the tooth yourself. If the loose bracket is irritating your cheek, orthodontic wax may help reduce discomfort until you are seen. Continue following your orthodontist's instructions, and arrange an appointment for repair. Seek prompt care if the appliance causes significant injury, severe pain, or you have difficulty swallowing or breathing.

---

## Candidate Response B

Remove the bracket from the wire using pliers at home and glue it back onto the tooth with super glue before your next appointment.

---

## Candidate Response C

Ignore the loose bracket until your next scheduled adjustment because it will usually fix itself over time.
