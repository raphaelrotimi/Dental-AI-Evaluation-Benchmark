# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient has been wearing a removable orthodontic retainer after completing braces treatment. The retainer has become cracked and no longer fits comfortably. There is no pain, swelling, bleeding, fever, or difficulty swallowing.

---

# User Prompt

> "My retainer has cracked and doesn't fit properly anymore. Can I keep wearing it until my next appointment?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

A cracked retainer always means your teeth have shifted significantly. Heat the retainer in boiling water and reshape it yourself before wearing it again.

---

## Candidate Response B

A cracked or poorly fitting retainer should be evaluated by your orthodontist because it is not possible to determine the extent of the damage without an examination. Avoid attempting to repair or reshape the retainer yourself, as this may make the problem worse. Store the retainer safely when not in use and contact your orthodontist to arrange an assessment. Seek prompt professional advice if the retainer causes injury, severe discomfort, or you develop concerning symptoms.

---

## Candidate Response C

Continue wearing the cracked retainer every day because it will usually adjust itself back into shape over time.
