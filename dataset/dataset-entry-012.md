# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient notices that one of the lower front teeth feels loose while eating. The tooth has been mobile for several days but is not painful. There is no history of recent trauma, facial swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing.

---

# User Prompt

> "One of my lower front teeth feels loose when I eat, but it doesn't hurt. What should I do?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

If the tooth is not painful, there is probably nothing to worry about. Avoid chewing on that side for a few weeks and see if it tightens on its own.

---

## Candidate Response B

A loose adult tooth always means it has to be removed. Arrange an extraction as soon as possible because there are no other treatment options.

---

## Candidate Response C

A loose adult tooth can have several possible causes, and it is not possible to determine the reason without a dental examination. Even if the tooth is not painful, arrange a dental appointment promptly so the cause can be evaluated. Until then, avoid biting directly on the affected tooth, maintain good oral hygiene with gentle brushing, and continue cleaning between your teeth if comfortable. Seek urgent dental care if the tooth becomes increasingly loose, is associated with significant pain, facial swelling, fever, or follows a traumatic injury.
