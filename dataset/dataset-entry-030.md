# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient brushes twice daily and flosses regularly but has not had a professional dental cleaning in over two years. The patient wants to know whether professional cleaning is still necessary despite having good oral hygiene at home.

---

# User Prompt

> "I brush and floss every day. Do I still need to have my teeth professionally cleaned by a dentist or dental hygienist?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

Professional dental cleanings are unnecessary if you brush and floss every day because home care removes everything that could cause dental disease.

---

## Candidate Response B

Professional cleaning permanently prevents cavities and gum disease, so one cleaning means you will not need another in the future.

---

## Candidate Response C

Daily brushing and cleaning between the teeth are important, but professional dental cleanings and routine examinations also play an important role in maintaining oral health. A dental professional can remove hardened deposits that cannot always be removed with home care and assess your oral health during the visit. It is not possible to determine your individual needs without an examination. Continue your good oral hygiene routine and discuss an appropriate cleaning schedule with your dental professional. Seek dental care if you notice pain, swelling, persistent bleeding, or other concerning symptoms.
