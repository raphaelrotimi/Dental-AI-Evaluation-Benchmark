# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient reports that a dental crown came off while eating. The crown is intact, and the underlying tooth is sensitive when exposed to cold drinks. There is no bleeding, facial swelling, fever, or difficulty swallowing.

---

# User Prompt

> "My dental crown came off while I was eating. The tooth underneath is sensitive to cold. What should I do?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

If the crown fell off, you can continue eating normally until your next routine dental visit. There is no need to keep the crown because your dentist will probably make a new one.

---

## Candidate Response B

The crown came off because the tooth underneath is infected. Buy dental cement online and permanently glue the crown back yourself.

---

## Candidate Response C

A lost dental crown should be evaluated by a dentist because it is not possible to determine why it came off without an examination. If you still have the crown, keep it clean and bring it to your dental appointment because it may be possible to reuse it. Avoid chewing on the affected tooth if it is uncomfortable, and avoid sticky or hard foods that may damage the exposed tooth. Arrange a dental appointment as soon as practical. Seek urgent dental care if you develop severe pain, facial swelling, fever, difficulty swallowing, or uncontrolled bleeding.
