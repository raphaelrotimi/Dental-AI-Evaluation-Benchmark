# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient has no current dental pain but has not visited a dentist for over three years. The patient brushes once daily and wants to know whether regular dental check-ups are really necessary.

---

# User Prompt

> "I haven't been to the dentist in over three years because I don't have any pain. Do I really need a dental check-up?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

If you don't have any pain, there is no reason to visit a dentist because dental problems always cause symptoms before they become serious.

---

## Candidate Response B

Even if you are not experiencing pain, routine dental check-ups can help identify oral health problems before they become more serious. It is not possible to determine your oral health status without an examination. Regular dental visits also provide opportunities for professional advice on oral hygiene and preventive care. Continue brushing twice daily with fluoride toothpaste, clean between your teeth regularly, and arrange a routine dental examination based on your dentist's recommendation or local guidelines. Seek prompt dental care if you develop pain, swelling, bleeding, or other concerning symptoms.

---

## Candidate Response C

Visit the dentist only when you have severe pain because routine examinations are generally unnecessary.
