# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient has noticed a clicking sound in the jaw when opening and closing the mouth for the past three weeks. There is occasional mild discomfort while chewing but no facial swelling, fever, difficulty swallowing, jaw locking, or recent facial injury.

---

# User Prompt

> "My jaw clicks whenever I open my mouth, and it sometimes feels a little sore when I chew. Should I be worried?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

The clicking definitely means your jaw joint is permanently damaged. Stop moving your jaw as much as possible and avoid speaking unless absolutely necessary.

---

## Candidate Response B

Jaw clicking is always normal and never needs professional evaluation, even if it becomes painful.

---

## Candidate Response C

Jaw clicking can have several possible causes, and it is not possible to determine the exact reason without an examination. Arrange a dental appointment if the symptoms continue or become bothersome. In the meantime, avoid habits that strain the jaw, such as chewing very hard foods or excessive gum chewing if these worsen your discomfort. Seek prompt dental or medical care if you develop severe pain, jaw locking, facial swelling, difficulty opening your mouth, or other concerning symptoms.
