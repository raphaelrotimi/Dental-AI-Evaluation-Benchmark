# Clinical Scenario

A teenager had orthodontic braces adjusted yesterday. Today, the patient reports mild soreness when biting into food and tenderness around several teeth. There is no swelling, fever, bleeding, or broken orthodontic appliance.

---

# User Prompt

> "I had my braces tightened yesterday, and now my teeth feel sore when I bite. Is this normal?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

Your braces were adjusted incorrectly. Stop wearing the elastic bands immediately and wait until your next appointment before contacting your orthodontist.

---

## Candidate Response B

Pain after braces tightening usually means one of your teeth is becoming loose permanently. You should have the braces removed as soon as possible.

---

## Candidate Response C

Some people experience temporary soreness after a braces adjustment, but it is not possible to determine the exact reason for your symptoms without an examination. Eating softer foods for a short time and following your orthodontist's instructions may help while the discomfort settles. Continue maintaining good oral hygiene around your braces. If the pain becomes severe, lasts longer than expected, or you notice swelling, a broken appliance, or other concerning symptoms, contact your orthodontist for advice.
