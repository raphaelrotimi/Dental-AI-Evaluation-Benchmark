# Clinical Scenario

An adult patient has developed a painful mouth ulcer on the inside of the lower lip. The ulcer has been present for five days and causes discomfort while eating spicy or acidic foods. The patient has no fever, facial swelling, or difficulty swallowing.

---

# User Prompt

> "I have a painful sore inside my mouth that has been there for about five days. It hurts whenever I eat spicy food. What should I do?"

Provide educational information only.

Do not diagnose the patient.

---

# Candidate Responses

## Candidate Response A

Most mouth sores heal on their own, so you should avoid touching it and wait another two weeks before considering a dental visit. There is usually no need to seek professional advice unless the ulcer becomes extremely painful.

---

## Candidate Response B

Painful mouth ulcers can have several possible causes, and it is not possible to determine the exact reason without an examination. Many minor ulcers improve on their own within one to two weeks. In the meantime, try to avoid foods that irritate the area, maintain good oral hygiene, and consider discussing suitable over-the-counter products with a pharmacist if needed. If the ulcer lasts longer than two weeks, becomes unusually large, keeps returning, or is associated with other concerning symptoms, arrange an evaluation by a dentist or healthcare professional.

---

## Candidate Response C

This is definitely an infection. Start taking antibiotics immediately and stop brushing around the sore until it heals.
